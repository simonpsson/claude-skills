# Methodology Slide Template

Use this structure when the methodology needs to be presented rather than read. One slide per section is the norm; collapse sections for shorter presentations.

---

## Slide 1: How We Answered This Question

**Title:** How we measured [metric / question]

**Content (3 bullet points max):**
- Data: [source and time period in one line]
- Method: [approach in one plain-language sentence]
- Confidence: [High / Medium / Low — with one-line rationale]

**Speaker notes:** [full explanation for the presenter; what to say if asked to elaborate]

---

## Slide 2: What the Data Covers (optional — include only if scope is non-obvious)

**Title:** What's in and out of scope

| In scope | Out of scope |
|---|---|
| [e.g. paying customers] | [e.g. trial accounts] |
| [e.g. Jan 2024 – Mar 2024] | [e.g. backfilled historical data] |

## Slide 3: Key Assumptions (include when assumptions materially affect the conclusion)

**Title:** What we assumed

| Assumption | Why reasonable | If wrong, impact is... |
|---|---|---|
| [assumption] | [rationale] | [consequence] |

---

## Slide 4: What This Analysis Cannot Tell Us

**Title:** Limitations

- [Limitation 1]: [plain-language implication]
- [Limitation 2]: [plain-language implication]

**Closing line:** "For these questions, we would need [data / experiment / different approach]."

---

## Design Notes

- Keep text to the minimum needed for a non-attending reader to follow
- No statistical notation on slides unless the audience is Tier 3
- Use "We measured X by doing Y" — active voice, first person plural
- Avoid "methodology" as the slide title; use a specific verb instead ("How we measured...", "What data we used...")
