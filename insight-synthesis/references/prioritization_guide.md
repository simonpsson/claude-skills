# Prioritization Guide for Analytical Insights

## Why prioritization is necessary

An analysis that presents 15 equally-weighted findings makes no decision easier. The analyst's job is to do the cognitive work of prioritization so the audience doesn't have to.

---

## Impact × Confidence matrix

Plot each insight on two axes:

- **Impact:** How large is the business effect? (revenue, users, cost, risk)
- **Confidence:** How certain are we? (data quality, assumption strength, sample size)

| | High confidence | Low confidence |
|---|---|---|
| **High impact** | Priority 1 — act now | Priority 2 — validate fast |
| **Low impact** | Priority 3 — background context | Priority 4 — omit |

Priority 4 insights should be cut from the deliverable. Including them dilutes the signal.

---

## Urgency overlay

After the impact × confidence sort, apply urgency:

- **Time-sensitive** (decision in < 1 week): Surface immediately, even if confidence is medium
- **Strategic** (decision in 1–3 months): Can wait for validation
- **Exploratory** (no current decision): Document for future reference; do not include in executive outputs

---

## Actionability filter

Before finalising the insight list, filter for actionability:

- **Actionable:** The audience can take a specific action that addresses this insight
- **Informational:** The audience can use this to calibrate their model but cannot act directly
- **Background:** Provides context but changes nothing

Lead with actionable insights. Include informational ones if they affect the credibility of the actionable ones. Cut background-only insights from the main narrative.

---

## Scoring rubric

Quick scoring for prioritization when you have many findings:

| Criterion | 1 | 2 | 3 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue / cost impact | < $10K | $10K–$100K | > $100K |
| Users / customers affected | < 1% | 1–10% | > 10% |
| Decision urgency | Months away | Within a month | This week |
| Confidence | Low | Medium | High |
| Actionability | None | Indirect | Direct |

Total score range: 5–15. Prioritise 12–15 first; present 8–11 as supporting; omit 5–7.

---

## Presenting the prioritization logic

Be explicit about what you prioritised and why. "The top three findings account for 85% of the revenue impact" is a justification, not just a claim.

If you cut findings, say so: "We identified seven areas of interest; the three below represent the highest-impact, highest-confidence opportunities."

This builds trust — it signals rigour, not selection bias.
